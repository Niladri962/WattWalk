<h1 align="center">WattWalk: Energy Producing Cycle Pedals using Piezoelectric Sensors</h1>

<p align="center">
Sustainable Energy Generation through Pedal-Powered Technology
</p>

---

## Project Overview

<p align="justify">
The increasing demand for sustainable and renewable energy solutions has encouraged the development of innovative technologies capable of harvesting energy from everyday human activities. One such activity is cycling, which involves continuous mechanical motion that can be converted into usable electrical energy. The WattWalk project focuses on designing an energy-producing cycle pedal system that utilizes piezoelectric sensors to convert mechanical pressure generated during pedaling into electrical energy.
</p>

<p align="justify">
Piezoelectric sensors have the unique ability to generate electrical charge when mechanical stress is applied to them. In this project, these sensors are strategically placed within the cycle pedal structure so that every pedal movement produces mechanical pressure on the sensors. This pressure generates electrical voltage, which is then collected, stored, and utilized to power small electronic devices or charge batteries. The system demonstrates an environmentally friendly approach to energy generation by harnessing human kinetic energy.
</p>

## Working Principle

<p align="justify">
The WattWalk system operates based on the piezoelectric effect, a phenomenon in which certain materials generate electrical charge when subjected to mechanical stress. When a cyclist applies force to the pedal, mechanical pressure is exerted on the embedded piezoelectric sensors. This pressure deforms the piezoelectric material, causing it to produce an electrical voltage.
</p>

<p align="justify">
The generated electrical signal is typically alternating in nature and must be conditioned through an electrical circuit. A rectifier circuit converts the alternating voltage into direct current (DC), which can then be stored in a rechargeable battery or capacitor. The stored energy can subsequently be used to power low-energy devices such as LED lights, sensors, or portable electronic gadgets.
</p>

## System Components

The WattWalk system consists of the following major components:

- **Piezoelectric Sensors** – Convert mechanical pressure into electrical energy.
- **Cycle Pedal Assembly** – Transfers mechanical force to the sensors.
- **Rectifier Circuit** – Converts AC output from sensors into DC power.
- **Energy Storage Unit** – Stores generated electricity in batteries or capacitors.
- **Voltage Regulator** – Stabilizes the electrical output.
- **Output Device** – Utilizes the generated electricity (LED lights, small electronics).

---

## System Architecture

<p align="justify">
The architecture of the WattWalk system includes mechanical, electrical, and energy storage components integrated into a single framework. When the cyclist pedals, mechanical force is transferred to the piezoelectric sensors embedded within the pedal structure. These sensors generate electrical voltage, which is passed through a rectification circuit to convert the output into usable direct current. The resulting energy is stored in a battery or capacitor and can be used to power various small electronic applications.
</p>

---

## Workflow

The operational workflow of the WattWalk system can be summarized as follows:

1. The cyclist applies force while pedaling the bicycle.
2. Mechanical pressure is transferred to the piezoelectric sensors.
3. Sensors convert mechanical stress into electrical voltage.
4. Generated AC voltage is sent to a rectifier circuit.
5. Rectifier converts the signal into usable DC power.
6. Electrical energy is stored in a battery or capacitor.
7. Stored energy is used to power small devices.
